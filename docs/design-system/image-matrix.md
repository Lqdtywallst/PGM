# PGM Image Matrix

Operational guide for selecting, approving and auditing images across Dynasty Prestige / PGM.

This document is a decision layer before changing assets. It does not define implementation details for HTML, CSS, JS or production data.

## 1. Guiding Principle

PGM imagery should sell a premium Dubai mobility experience, not a generic luxury car catalogue.

Every image must answer one clear question:

- What is the user deciding on this surface?
- Does the image make that decision feel easier, calmer and more premium?
- Does it reinforce Dubai, concierge quality, fleet credibility or model desirability?

The preferred brand direction is cinematic, calm, high-end and service-led:

- Warm Dubai light, deep blacks, champagne/gold undertones and controlled highlights.
- Clean compositions with generous negative space for text.
- Real vehicle presence, but not always maximum car drama.
- Concierge and handover context where the page is about service.
- Consistent crop logic across cards and page families.

Images should avoid the two weak extremes:

- Generic stock luxury: models, glass towers, fake smiles, obvious staged business scenes.
- Dealer catalogue chaos: random angles, random backgrounds, mismatched color grades and no shared brand mood.

## 3. Asset Selection Rules

### Framing

- Hero images need enough negative space for headline and primary CTA.
- Fleet and vehicle cards should show the full exterior when used as primary media.
- Interior images are support media unless the page is explicitly about cabin comfort, chauffeur or monthly use.
- People are allowed only when they show service quality, not stock lifestyle posing.
- Dubai context should be real or convincingly local: Palm, Marina, Downtown, hotel/villa, airport, premium roads.

### Ratio

- Home and page heroes: prefer 16:9 or wider, with usable crop at 390x844, 768x1024, 1366x768 and 1920x1080.
- Cards: prefer 1.45-1.8 landscape. Avoid portrait crops as primary card media.
- Category stage assets can be wider, but vehicle scale must remain comparable.
- PDP gallery can include portrait/detail crops, but hero and first gallery item should remain responsive-safe.

### Color Grade

- Target a shared PGM grade: warm blacks, champagne highlights, controlled contrast, low color cast.
- Avoid strong green garden backgrounds unless the whole series is intentionally resort-led.
- Avoid cold blue showroom lighting unless it is Porsche/precision-led and balanced by the rest of the page.
- Avoid over-saturated red/yellow vehicles dominating the page unless the page is brand-specific and text is protected.

### Overlay

- Text over image/video must always pass contrast and should sit on a designed gradient, not accidental darkness.
- Hero overlay guideline: 45-65% effective darkness where text appears.
- Card overlay guideline: minimal, only enough for label/readability.
- If an image needs an extreme overlay to work, the asset is probably wrong for that surface.

### Safe Area

- For hero surfaces, reserve a text-safe zone with no strong subject, no bright sky patch and no hard vertical line behind the headline.
- Desktop safe area should normally protect the left or center-left content column.
- Mobile safe area must protect the top 45-60% of the viewport depending on headline position.
- Do not place CTAs over wheels, grille, headlamps or high-contrast city lights.

### Mobile Crop

- Validate every key image at mobile, tablet, laptop and desktop before approval.
- Mobile must not crop off wheels, roofline, car nose, person head, map marker labels or important service gestures.
- If `object-fit: cover` hides the subject on mobile, the image needs a different crop or a dedicated mobile asset.

## 6. Future Automated Visual Auditor Rules

The future auditor should validate both technical quality and brand fit.

### Deterministic Rules

- Contrast: text over image must meet WCAG 4.5:1 for normal text and 3:1 for large text.
- Saturation: warn if mean saturation is above 0.45 for hero/card images unless explicitly brand-approved.
- Luminance: warn if text-zone luminance is too high for white text or too low for dark text.
- Ratio: flag primary card images outside 1.45-1.8 and hero images without a usable 16:9 crop.
- Repetition: warn if the same asset appears as a primary hero/card in more than two page families.
- Semantics: compare file name, alt text and page use; flag interior/exterior mismatch.
- Mobile crop: validate subject visibility at 390x844 and 768x1024.
- Safe area: sample the headline bounding box area and flag high-contrast edges or bright patches behind text.
- Brand risk: flag visible third-party text, license plates when not intended, watermarks and non-Dubai landmarks.
- Card crop: detect clipped wheels, roofline, grille or car nose on primary vehicle cards.

### Human Review Rules

- Does the image feel owned or at least carefully curated?
- Does it communicate the page decision faster than text alone?
- Does it feel like Dubai/UAE where location matters?
- Does it match PGM premium calm rather than generic supercar noise?
- Would this image still feel premium if viewed next to the rest of the same page family?

## 7. New Image Approval Checklist

Before adding or replacing any image, confirm:

- The asset has a clear surface role: hero, card, gallery, category, service, location or support.
- The image is owned, licensed or otherwise legally usable.
- It contains no visible watermark, third-party rental brand, unrelated city, misleading landmark or unwanted plate detail.
- The crop works at mobile, tablet, laptop and desktop.
- The subject is not accidentally clipped in the intended component.
- Text safe area is documented if the image will sit behind copy.
- Color grade aligns with PGM: warm, premium, controlled saturation and consistent blacks.
- The image does not duplicate an existing primary asset unless the reuse is intentional.
- Alt text accurately describes the visual and does not claim exterior/interior/service context incorrectly.
- The image helps the page decision: browse fleet, pick service, pick location, trust team, reserve, or inspect vehicle.
- If the image is a competitor/brand reference, it is recorded as inspiration only and not treated as an asset solution.

## Notes On Inspiration Versus Usable Assets

Competitor or premium-brand references can be used to discuss framing, hierarchy and mood only.

They should not be copied into the site or proposed as direct replacements unless there is a valid license/source path. PGM should prefer owned shoots, licensed marketplace assets with clear terms, or generated/commissioned visuals that are sufficiently distinct and legally safe.
